About

How to read Castia Market

Castia Market is a price tracker, not a trading authority. It summarizes current auction data so players can judge offers with more context.

Prices

Median is the middle observed price after current seller listings are sorted. Auction House and Chest Shop data are calculated separately.

Price range shows the usual middle band of listings for the selected source. A wider range generally means the market is less consistent or has fewer useful samples.

Samples means distinct current seller offers used for the selected source, not every historical relist ever scraped. Older Auction-style rows with unknown source are grouped with Auction House data.

Seller labels

Seller labels are automatic current-state signals based on recent pricing behavior. Auction House and Chest Shop labels are separate because those economies can behave differently.

  • Trustworthy: usually priced close to the current market median.
  • Neutral: mixed behavior or too little signal to classify strongly.
  • Suspicious: often priced meaningfully above the market median.
  • Flagged: strongest automatic warning from current pricing behavior.
  • Blacklisted: automatic current-state warning surfaced separately by the scoring system.

Why the system is not harsher

Very cheap items are ignored for seller ratings because doubling a five-dollar item is not economically comparable to overpricing expensive gear.

Underpriced listings count more gently than overpriced ones. Players often undercut to help newer players or sell faster, so generosity should not damage reputation as much as aggressive overpricing.

No-history items

Known set gear remains visible in Listings even when no current market history exists. Those rows are useful for enchantment reference and comparison, but they do not invent prices.
